This paper presents a comparison of several statistical models and machine learning techniques, as well as the creation of buy and hold systems, for the prediction of Bitcoin price. A review of cryptocurrencies, time series, statistical techniques and machine learning was conducted to compare the models and propose the systems. The proposed methodology used the closing price of Bitcoin for both periodicities and statistical and machine learning techniques as predictors. Different ways to evaluate the results were presented in an intrinsic and extrinsic manner. The results show that the systems obtained better results for the hourly periodicity, with the SES (1H) model standing out with a balance of $235.66, 54.82% accuracy and 0.54 f1-score. Other noteworthy cases include PROPHET (1H) with a balance of $90.67, 50.84% accuracy and 0.56 f1-score, SES (1D) with a balance of $157.36, 55.93% accuracy and 0.56 f1-score, and MA (1H) with a balance of $201.51, 54.11% accuracy and 0.54 f1-score.
